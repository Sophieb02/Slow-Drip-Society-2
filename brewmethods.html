Brew Methods

Pour-Over

Precise • Clean • Ritual-focused

Pour-over highlights clarity and nuance. Coffee grounds sit in a filter while hot water is poured in slow circles, allowing you to control flow, timing, and extraction.

This method is ideal for single-origin coffees and moments when you want brewing to become a meditative, hands-on ritual.

French Press

Full-bodied • Immersive • Unrushed

French press uses full immersion brewing. Coffee grounds steep directly in hot water, then are pressed down with a metal filter.

It’s forgiving, simple, and great for sharing. Perfect for slow weekend mornings and communal cups.

AeroPress

Compact • Portable • Experiment-friendly

AeroPress is a playful, flexible brewer. Using air pressure and short brew times, it can create espresso-like shots or clean, filter-style cups.

Compact, portable, and endlessly experimentable.

Pressure-based extraction in under two minutes.

A tool that rewards curiosity and iteration.

Cold Brew

Slow • Smooth • Make-ahead

Cold brew steeps coarse grounds in cold water for 12–24 hours. The result is a low-acid, smooth concentrate that can be served over ice or with milk.

It’s a set-and-forget brew — great for planning ahead and having coffee ready for busy days.

Espresso

Intense • Fast • Precision-driven

Espresso uses high pressure to push hot water through a compact puck of finely ground coffee, producing a concentrated shot with rich crema.

It’s technical and highly sensitive to grind, dose, and timing — a perfect metaphor for design decisions that demand precision.
